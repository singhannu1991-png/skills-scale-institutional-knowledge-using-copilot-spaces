# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

## Overview

OctoAcme uses a structured, iterative project management approach centered on customer value, clear ownership, and data-informed decisions. Our processes guide teams through the complete project lifecycle—from initial concept through execution, release, and retrospective.

### What is OctoAcme's Project Management Approach?

OctoAcme follows a **customer-first, data-driven methodology** built for cross-functional teams delivering product features, services, and integrations. The approach is grounded in five core principles:

- **Customer-first**: Prioritize customer value and usability in all decisions
- **Iterative delivery**: Deliver small, testable increments rather than large, infrequent releases
- **Clear ownership**: Each project has a named Project Manager (PM) and Product Lead to drive accountability
- **Data-informed decisions**: Measure impact and iterate based on evidence
- **Psychological safety**: Encourage feedback, experimentation, and learning from failures

### Key Characteristics

**Structured Lifecycle**: Every project moves through a defined five-phase lifecycle (Initiation → Planning → Execution → Release → Retrospective) with decision gates, checklists, and clear artifacts at each stage. This ensures alignment and reduces surprises.

**Defined Roles & Communication**: OctoAcme operates with complementary roles—Project Managers coordinate delivery and mitigate risks, Product Managers define outcomes and prioritize work, Developers build and collaborate on design and testing, and QA ensures quality. Weekly PM/PdM syncs, twice-weekly team standups, and monthly stakeholder updates maintain transparency and alignment.

**Quality & Risk Management**: Work is tracked on project boards with a clear Definition of Done. Pull requests are kept small (≤400 lines) with automated CI/CD checks, security scanning, and at least one approval required. Pre-release requirements include staging deployment, smoke tests, and documented rollback plans. A Risk Register is maintained and reviewed weekly, with escalation paths for blockers.

**Continuous Improvement**: After each sprint, release, or milestone, teams run structured retrospectives to capture "what went well," "what could improve," and actionable next steps with owners and due dates. This cycle of reflection and refinement enables teams to reduce cycle time and optimize processes over time.

## Project Lifecycle

1. **Initiation** → Problem statement, stakeholders, high-level timeline
2. **Planning** → Scope, resources, milestones, dependencies
3. **Execution** → Build, test, review, iterate
4. **Release** → Deploy, verify, announce
5. **Close & Retrospective** → Capture learnings and next steps

## Key Roles

- **Project Manager (PM)**: Coordinates delivery, schedules, risks, communications
- **Product Manager (PdM)**: Defines outcomes, prioritizes backlog, measures success
- **Developers**: Implement features, collaborate on design and testability
- **QA/Testing**: Validates quality and acceptance criteria
- **Stakeholders**: Provide inputs and approvals
